Statistics is a fundamental part of our vocation. We live it and breathe it, so to speak.

Thus, interesting books on statistics tend to catch my attention. And when they are also freely available, even better :-) (Although you can buy a copy of it, and help the authors out.)

One such book worth a read is: The Elements of Statistical Learning: Data Mining, Inference, and Prediction.

Although you can buy a physical copy of the book, the authors of the book (Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani and Jerome Friedman) have also made the book available to download.
